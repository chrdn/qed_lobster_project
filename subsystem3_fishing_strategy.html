Fishing Strategy

Operational tactics and crew management

Subsystem Description:

The Fishing Strategy Subsystem models the profitability of fishing for 3 different species. This subsystem selects which fishing strategies to complete on which days of the year based on the seasons and regulations. It additionally explores the potential increases in productivity that could occur as a result of the crew receiving a bottom-line cut of the boat’s profits. This subsystem allows us to decide how much to pay the fishermen, how large of a bonus they should receive, what is fished on what day, and most importantly the annual revenue of the boat.

Subsystem Modeling:

The design variables include revenue stream toggles, crew bonus, and sales strategy (wholesale vs. direct). The objective function computes total revenue minus all daily expenses, including the cost of fuel, labor, and bonuses dependent on the fishing strategy. Although constraints weren’t explicitly added, the use of the log function results in a realistic representation of a plateau in profitability in spite of an increase in wages. This prevented the model from selecting the highest bonus leading to very realistic outputs for both the revenue and crew bonuses.

Subsystem Optimization:

We used MATLAB's fmincon function to implement a quasi-newton optimization method to determine the productivity of the crew. Using the log functions to simulate the productivity plateau and upper and lower bounds to the crew bonus we ended up with a very realistic outcome. The best solution achieved a net profit of over $7M and involved the crew getting a 10% bonus for each fishing strategy. The optimal fishing strategy had the boat fish for lobster 10.31% of the time, scallops 8.91% of the time, and cod 10.48% of the time.

Subsystem Analysis of Solution:

The optimized solution effectively balanced wages and productivity. The newton-method chose a crew bonus that was justified given the increase in productivity. It also prioritized lobster fishing when overlapping fishing seasons occurred, showing that the yield and market price was enough to counteract the cost of higher wages, more working hours and higher fuel costs. The results demonstrate that lobster fishing is the most profitable of the 3 options, but a combination of the 3 is superior to one single strategy. MATLAB was used for all modeling and optimization.

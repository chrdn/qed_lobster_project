Business Model

Financial analysis and revenue optimization

Subsystem Description

The Business Subsystem models the financial viability of a commercial fishing operation. It explores tradeoffs between revenue streams (lobster sales, other fish sales, and off-season charter rental) and costs (boat purchase, maintenance, licensing, labor, and loan structure). We chose this subsystem to evaluate how financing, sales strategy, and asset utilization affect long-term profit, helping inform decisions at the business strategy level. A key focus was whether to prioritize wholesale or direct-to-customer sales, and how to allocate limited fishing and rental time to maximize profitability.

Subsystem Modeling:

We developed a detailed mathematical model that simulates the business operation over a season. The design variables include revenue stream toggles, maintenance interval, loan term, capital cost, licensing cost, rental usage, and sales strategy (wholesale vs. direct). The objective function computes total revenue minus all associated costs, including a realistic labor cost if direct-to-customer sales are selected. Constraints were added to enforce a maximum rental limit, minimum license cost, and budgetary limits on the boat purchase. The model also includes a soft penalty to discourage unrealistic configurations such as minimum maintenance or license costs.

Subsystem Optimization:

We used MATLAB's Genetic Algorithm to optimize our business model. The GA evaluated 10 variables, including 5 binary/integer decisions. It was configured with a population size of 500 and a max of 300 generations. The best solution achieved a net profit of over $7M and recommended using all available time: lobster fishing, fish sales, and 60 days of rental charters. The optimal strategy involved selling wholesale to avoid labor costs, using the cheapest boat possible, and minimizing loan term and maintenance interval.

Subsystem Analysis of Solution:

The optimized solution effectively balanced profitability and feasibility. The GA chose a strategy that filled the entire available season, prioritized simplicity via wholesale sales, and minimized costs wherever possible. However, the tendency to minimize maintenance and licensing suggests that the realism penalty may need to be strengthened. The results demonstrate that all three revenue streams contribute significantly to profit when well-coordinated, and validate the usefulness of the Genetic Algorithm in exploring high-dimensional tradeoffs. MATLAB was used for all modeling and optimization.

Subsystem 2: Boat Selection

Subsystem Description

Selecting the right lobster boat involves balancing competing priorities: fuel efficiency, storage capacity, seaworthiness, and cost. This subsystem models these tradeoffs using real-world data to replicate the decision-making process of a commercial fisherman. For instance, larger boats can carry more lobsters and gear but require bigger engines and consume more fuel. Similarly, hull design impacts performance in different ocean depthsâ€”planar hulls excel in shallow waters, while downeast hulls handle rougher, deeper seas but at a higher price. The subsystem's goal is to minimize annual fuel costs while ensuring the boat meets minimum capacity requirements and stays within budget constraints for both upfront purchase (<$1.8M) and annual maintenance (<$180K)
